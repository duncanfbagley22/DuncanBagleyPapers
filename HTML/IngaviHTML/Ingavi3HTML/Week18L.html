Week 17 Mission President Letter

Weekly Letter - 15 Dic 2014

Dear President Hansen,
    It's really weird that my time in the office is coming to an end. It has gone by really fast and I´ve learned a ton. I wasn't super excited to be secretary at first, but I know that it is where I needed to be. We didn't get to work a ton in the field this week, but we did have a few good lessons. We talked to Andres yesterday and he's still doing great. We have a family that seemed really good, but they weren't able to come to church yesterday. Actually, the mom showed up during the sacrament when the doors were closed. She then left before we could talk to her in person. We ran out and called her, but she said she felt bad that she showed up late and left because of that. My heart just sank in those moments. I really hope that the family can keep progressing, because they are the type of family that needs the church, and the church needs them. As far as my new area and companion, I am super excited. I already know Elder Laidlaw pretty well and he is a spectacular missionary. I know that we'll work hard and get stuff done. I know that I'm going to learn a lot from him. Honestly I was hoping to go to some city in the mission that I hadn't been to yet, but when you told me my change I new it was right. I realized that I'm not here to travel Bolivia, but to work as a missionary, whever the Lord needs me. Thank you for everything, and let me know if there is anything you need me to do.
    
    Elder Bagley

President Hansen Response

Thank you, thank you, thank you for your excellent and dedicated work as mission CFO. What a blessing for me to arrive with such a solid financial secretary.
I had a very sweet impression in prayer to assign you as a ZL with Elder Laidlaw, so I know you are where you are supposed to be for now.
