Week 7 Mission President Letter

Weekly Letter - 29 Sep 2014

Dear President Hansen,
    This was a very busy week for us. The transfers are always pretty hectic for us. At the end of the week we are usually pretty exhausted, as was the case this time. I do love the transfers week though. Meeting all the new missionaries is really fun and they always bring a good energy to the mission. It always changes up the routine a little bit which is good. I´ve also enjoyed the change in the offices. I´ll miss having Elder Weekes and Elder Prado around, but it´s good to get some new people in here. Elder Mendoza is a solid missionary, and is learning very quickly how to do everything in the office. He is also very obedient, and will have a good impact on the office, especially on his companion Elder Calderon. This week was a little bit disappointing in some aspects. From talking with Elder Jacobson (he´s in Churuquella 2, my first area), I learned that the majority of the people that I either baptized or rescued in that area are inactive. It´s hard to hear that people I thought had progressed so much have fallen back down again. I´m going to try to get in contact with those people though, because I know that they have strong testimonies. Yesterday we had a pretty cool experience. Walking down the street we just greeted everyone that went by like usual, when this lady stopped us. She told us that she was looking for someone to help her boyfriend stop drinking, and decided to ask us. We explained what we could do and set up an appointment for this morning. They werent there, but we´ll be persistent. I love meeting people that are really looking for something, because we know that if they follow the gospel they can find what they´re looking for. Thank you for all that you do, and thanks again for lunch on Saturday. Let me know if there is anything I can do for you guys.
    
    Elder Bagley

President Hansen Response
