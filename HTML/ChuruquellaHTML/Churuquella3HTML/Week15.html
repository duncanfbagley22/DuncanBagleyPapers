Week 15 (November 11, 2013)

One is the loneliest number...

Dear Fambam,
Well, I am back safe and sound in Sucremanta. This week was so much faster than the last week. I am sure the title is probably sparking some interest... So this is what happened. Monday, after I had p day in Cocha with Elder Lopez which was awesome, we found out I was coming back tuesday morning. Supayabe however, is on his way to his house right now cause he has to go back to get healthy. He wont come back until the next cambio, beginning of december. So that means... I am the only missionary in the whole mission without a companion. It is... different...hard... and ya... We have an odd number of missionaries, so I just have to wait, and wait, and wait. Tuesday and Wednesday I was with an elder on his way to Potosi, but had to wait cause there was blockeos, no sure how that translates, but it is when little pueblos block the roads in protest. Wednesday was all day service. First, as a zone, we went to a place to help plant potatoes. That includes scraping up 6 inches of manure that smelled horrible. Thursday I got to go with Elder Herndon and Elder Cusiatao, the zone leaders, as they came to help revive my area. It was fun to learn from them, and actually get some work done. Friday I went with Elder Long and Elder Diazgranados. Not a lot happened, until like 9 o´clock. We had to wait outside for a bit, cause everyone has to drop me off with Elder Espina and Harris, cause they are like my babysitters. We were waiting, and this teenager and her little brother came over and sat by us. The little kid was playing with a basketball, so naturally, that drew my interest. I started playing with him, and showing off a little, but mostly playing. I ended up talking to the girl, and we (not sure who we are) will go visit this week. Saturday was stake conference. President Dyer and President Jensen (Jay E Jensen) who is the new temple president spoke. It was great. Sunday, I got to go on splits with Hector. It wasnt too eventful, except for the drunk guy that stopped us. It was quite the experience with the drunk guy. He was mad I was white, kissed hector´s head, had us stare at the sky for like 5 minutes, wanted to have me kneel down and kiss a stone to show my humility, and then started to cry. Just a fun day. It was cool to go with Hector though, just 3 months ago we found him, and now he comes with us. Well, as far as companions go... I could literally get one any day. President Dyer told me there is someone coming, they have his name and everything, they just dont know when. It will either be someone who was waiting for their visa, or someone from the CCM, meaning I would have to train. As I am typing, there is a big protest in the street, so we cant leave for a while. They are shooting off fireworks and stuff and yelling for who knows what. Oh well, life is good. It will go a lot faster if I get a companion, but still good.
Hope everything is good at home. Send my love to erbody, and stay strong in the gospel.

Love you all, talk to you next week.
Chau
Elder Bagley
