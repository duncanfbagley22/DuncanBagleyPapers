Week 18 Mission President Letter

Weekly Letter - 02 Dic 2013

President Dyer,
    I am sorry I wasn´t able to write to you the last week. The internet wouldnt let me in. This week, as well as the last week, were pretty good. We had a lot of moments where we had to knock doors, but we took a different approach. Elder Pinta and I were usually just picking a few doors here and there, but this time we decided to knock them all, even if we thought they wouldnt want anything. With that technique, it felt like everything we touched turned to gold. Everybody would open, even people starting talking to us on the street. We found a few people that could progress. Me and Elder Pinta are getting along great. We really dont have any problems. I was glad I didn´t get transferred, as I feel like there are some important things we are going to do this cambio. We have one investigator that is really close to baptism. He just needs a stronger testimony of the gospel and of baptism, and we are working on that now. We have taught all the lessons, we just dont want to baptize him if he isnt ready. At times the mission is hard. I feel like I should be finding more success and I compare myself to others too much. That is something I need to fix. Overall, I am excited for this cambio, and I feel like we could find some success. After the few weeks I had without work due to being in Cochabamba and without a companion, the area was basically dead. It is starting to come back to life, and I feel like soon it could be a really strong area. Thanks for all that you do, and I am loving the mission. Hasta Lunes
    
    Elder Bagley

President Dyer Response

Thanks for trying not to compare yourself with others. You are unique. You are talented in a way NOBODY else is talented. You alone can reach some people.

Le felicito su exito. El Señor nos esta bendiciendo y Él tiene mucha confianza en usted. Favor de acercarse a su Obispo. Hay una persona para bautizar en cada familia menos activo. El mejor regalo que pudieramos dar al Señor este Navidad sería una persona nueva bautizada o rescatada.

Le queremos mucho. Que Él Señor le bendiga.

Presidente Dyer
