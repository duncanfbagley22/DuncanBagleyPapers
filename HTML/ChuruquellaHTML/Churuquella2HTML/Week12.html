Week 12 (October 21, 2013)

I´m going through changes (pretty sure that is a line from a song)

Dearest family,
How goes it back home? I am doing swell. First, I will talk about the thing that is on everyone´s mind...Cambios. This is whats up. I am staying in my area, so no change there. Elder Lopez is headed up to Cocha, he is going to train again, and he is going to open an area. He leaves Tuesday morning. It´s gonna be really weird to have a different companion. My new comp is... drumroll... Elder Supayape from Santa Cruz again. I am going to know more about Santa Cruz than Cochabamba. I really know nothing about Supayape (soo pa ya pay). Hopefully he turns out good. We will see wednesday. Finally I am a regular missionary, not being trained or anything. Other significant change is that Elder Cedano is leaving to Cocha too. He is our current DL and is going to be comps with my buddy Elder Harwood from the CCM. Not sure what his replacements name is, but apparently he is a bit flojo, lazy. Elder Long will be his comp, and he is perfect for lazy people. That is all for cambios, so you can rest for 6 weeks. Other things that happened this week that were good.
English Classes Me and Elder Long finally started teaching our english classes, tuesday night. 7 people showed up and it went descent enough. Now the goal is just transition into actually teaching the people lessons. Saturday we had another english class, this time in the morning. One of Elder Longs investigators is an english teacher, so she invited the whole class. We had about 20 teenage girls there to learn english, and I think it went well. Teaching takes a lot out of me, and it gave me a lot more respect for what Rachel does.
Less Actives We have been really actually pretty good with less actives. Right now we are focused on 3 families.
Daniela and Alison 2 little girls that just moved in upstairs from our house. They were baptized a year ago, and just moved to Sucre, so they hadnt found the church. They seem like they´ll keep going and be solid Young Women
Familia Plaza This is one of my favorite families in the ward. We teach them a lot, and they probably pretty much are active now, so that is good. Elder Lopez had a hard time leaving them last night cause we are so close. They are super humble and just love us.
Javier He is the younger brother of the 2nd counselor. He is super cool. He is a pro dancer and teaches dancing school. He is super humble and knows it is true, and tells us all the time. He has a kid with his ´´wife´´ more or less, but they dont live together. She is very catholic, but maybe with time she´ll open up.

As far as investigators go, here are the really good ones right now. Dennys He is hector´s friend and has come to church the last 4 or 5 times. He likes us, but is just waiting to receive his answer. He will get baptized eventually, it is just a matter of time. We are basically just waiting till he gets the answer he needs that it is the true church.
Nancy and Roberto They are viejos, meaning they are old. They are super catholic, but they really like us. At first they just liked that we talk about the bible, but now they are starting to get the real point. We have noticed that Nancy has taken down all her little figurines of little saints and baby jesus and the virgen. The problem with them is that they really dont wanna leave the catholic church, as it is part of their life. I think they know it is true, they just need a little push. We have been chipping away and it looks like we could get somewhere.
Roxana She is the lady we ran into with the miracle when Lopez was walking like crazy to end up at that house. She has a tumor, but doesnt know if it is cancerous or not. She is really humble and says we are angels. Again, super catholic. We will keep working with her and hopefully she gets somewhere.

That is it for the work. Our 2nd ward mission leader just moved, so now we are looking for a 3rd. We did go to that castle thing last week. I will attach pictures later of it. It was cool and we even left a pamphlet with the tour guide. Today we went to 7 cascadas (waterfalls). It was pretty cool. Me Elder Long Elder Harris and Elder Cedano hiked a bit farther up to see more of the waterfalls. It was so hard not to jump in the water, cause it was super blue and today was hot. Anyways, that is it for this week. I finished up 4 months last week, and also my 3 cambio overall. Hopefully, me and Supayape will get some more work done and big things will start happening in Churuquella. That is all for this week.

Que les vayan bien (that is what you say when you leave people)
Elder Bagley

P.S We found that we had a mouse running around the apartment, so last night we set out to kill it. Lopez finally got it with a broom stick somehow. I basically hate all animals now aside from pigs
