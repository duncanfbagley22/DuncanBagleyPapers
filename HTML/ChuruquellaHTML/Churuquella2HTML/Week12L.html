Week 12 Mission President Letter

Weekly Letter - 21 Oct 2013

President Dyer,
    This week felt a lot better. We didnt have quite as many lessons as we would´ve liked, but the ones that we did have felt pretty good. I was nervous that we were going to have a bad week, with cambios and all, but it was actually pretty good. We worked hard and had some blessings come our way. I feel as though the ward is just about ready to explode with some success. The members are starting to understand the fact that we need them, and hopefully that helps us moving forward. It really is the only way that we are going to get anywhere. As far as cambios go, we were expecting that one of us would leave, so nothing was a shock. I am so grateful for the time that I have had with Elder Lopez. The time didn´t feel like I was assigned to him, more that we were just really good friends. I learned so much from him. The way he formed relationships with the members made me realize that although we will bounce around from area to area throughout the mission, we are still teaching people, not lessons. That being said, the relationships with the members that Elder Lopez had made me realize that I need to form the same strong relations, so that members trust me and will work with me. Elder Lopez is a great missionary and will be great wherever he goes. As far as a new companion, I am excited and nervous. I am excited to get the chance to learn from someone else, but I am nervous that maybe he wont want to work, or we wont get along, or something like that. I trust however that Elder Supayape, I think his name is, was assigned to me through revelation, so I can trust that this is the plan the Lord has. I am excited to get working again in Churuquella, and hopefully soon big things will start to happen. Thanks for all that you do.
    
    Elder Bagley

President Dyer Response

You will like Elder Supayape. Gracias por su diligencia. Ruego al Señor que le bendiga.

Presidente Dyer
