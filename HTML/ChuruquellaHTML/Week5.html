Week 5 (September 2, 2013)

Por fin, Agosto está terminado

Mi familia,
Comó estamos? Whew, this week felt long and short at the same time. It is like during the week it feels like I am never gonna see the end, but right now it is weird that I finished it. Anyways, this week was definitely the most successful. In my first 4 weeks, we had one investigator at church in total... but yesterday we had five. Hector, and 4 of the 5 of the familia Leon. It was great. We were late cause we were trying to get the Leons to come, but we made it. It was fast sunday too, but you probably know that. Anyways, it was great. Hector sat with his mom, who was a member, so she helped him. We sat with the Leon family. After 2 testimonies, Gorki, 12, turned and asked me if he could go up. I said why not. He is gonna be prophet one day. His testimony is crazy. One day we went to find his family, and only he was home. We decided to teach him and challenge him to read. He described to us later his experience. He prayed with all his heart, all his conviction, and he said it was something special. He is gonna be important in the church somewhere. His dad is great too. Kinda slow if you understand me... but he is great. We challenged him to read the BoM intro, and he ended up reading half of First Nephi. It was great. We are definitely expect them all to be baptized. Hector is coming along great. He is just so happy about church stuff. He went from not believing in God to coming to church and reading the Bom in like 10 days. I know he will get baptized. We were able to watch the Joseph Smith movie with him and his family and it was great. He said he usually only likes action movies, but this one he liked. He is just so happy about the church. The family and him are our best investigators right now. We have some others that are good, but those are the best right now. I am gonna go through the week again... Monday- we had a ping pong tourney with both zones. I took 4th somehow. There are a lot of guys here that are really good. Like really good
Cant remember all the days, whoops, but here are the important events of the week
1. We were hanging out in Rumi Rumi one day, walking up a hill, and a lady turns to us and said she needed us, needed help from God. We went with her and she just kept saying how great we were. She wanted a type of bible or something... like a BoM. It was a great miracle, especially since that day we were down cause of few new investigators.
2. I got a closet, or dresser. It is a ropero in spanish. Life is easier now.
3. Camila, hurt her ankle. She is the youngest daughter of our pensionista. Like Joey´s ankle, but a bit better. She is still super happy though.
4. We finally started knocking doors this week, cause of the lack of investigators. It was cool cause we switch off picking doors, and after a few misses, I picked a random house and we have a return appointment on friday. At times I don´t trust that I have the spirit, but it is moments like that that help
5. Saturday we went to Hector´s pre military graduation. Super long, but it was good to be there with him. It was cool to see how it worked, but I now know why Bolivia lost to Chile in a war in one day...
6. Sunday we had a lesson with Miguel Gottlieb. He speaks Spanish, English, and German. He is a little weird, but smart. We taught like 2 lessons, I taught one in English, and then we taught one in Spanish. Cool stuff. He said it felt good to talk to us.
7. Elder Lopez has no concept of time...
8. Guess who was sleep talking in Spanish... This guy. Lopez says I was spouting off spanish in my sleep. Weird, cause I dont feel like I know that much. Cool stuff though.
9. We have been using the dog dazer. It is fun
I am trying to think of more to say. I am really bad at answering questions. I am still working on getting adjusted, but there are moments that make the mission worth it. I am glad to be done with august. Almost done with the first transfer, one more week. I am hoping to get more gringos in here. Oh well, that will be all for now. Baring my testimony in English helped me realize again why I am here.
Tinkuna Cama Familia
Love,
Elder Bagley
