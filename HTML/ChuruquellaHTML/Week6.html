Week 6 (September 9, 2013)

1 down, 15 to go

Fam bam,
Como estás? This week was definitely the fastest one that I have had. Not to say that it was fast, but I wasnt constantly looking down at my watch to see what time it was. I am trying to be more engaged as opposed to going through the motions. Anyways, as the title says, 1 down, 15 to go. I finished my first cambio (transfer). Finally I feel like I have made some progress. As expected, I am staying in Sucre. It was 110% sure that I was staying. Lopez is staying too, so no change there. Here is the layout of Sucre. There are 2 zones, 4 districts. Each district has 8 people. We always do stuff as all of sucre though. In our district, the only person leaving is Elder Mickle, the other gringo that lives with us. This means that probably we´ll get a newbie in the apartment, so I wont be the newest anymore. Other than that, the only change is that our zone leader is going, and his replacement was the secretary for the president, so he knows what he is doing. Here are some other things that happened this week.
1. The package came. It was great to get that t shirt. It is awesome. The food is also good. I had pop tarts and milk for breakfast one day, just like the good old days.
2. I gave my first blessing in spanish... I give myself a B-, but it isnt my spanish that counts, it is the blessing. It was to Gabriel, a lil 9 year old kid that is cool. He had an ear infection though, so we gave him a blessing. I sealed the annointing and stuff. It was cool
3. My first really unsuccessful day was wednesday. We walked around Rumi Rumi for 3 hours, and didnt teach a lesson. It was really dissappointing, but those things happen in the mission
4. We went to teach Ataualpa and Claudia, the people that the police are looking for, again. They were fairly normal, but you can tell that something is bugging them, like the law...
5. It is weird, even at the CCM, I didnt really like scripture study, but now I love it. I was reading in Alma the other day, and Alma 26 is like a victory speech by Ammon i think, and it is awesome. I read it twice just cause. Alma 17 11 is like a mission starting scripture, and Alma 26 is good to finish it.
6. I get pretty cranky if I am not in bed by 10 30. Lopez doesnt quite grasp time, so there are times where we dont finish planning until 10 or 1030, and I am not in a good mood. I need to try to be happier though. }
7. We found a new family on Sunday. Actually, we had some solid success in tracting this week. 9 new investigators is the most we have had. We will see how it all goes. The new family is great. They were a reference from Hma Sandra, a recent convert. Her, maria, and Hector came with us to teach. It is great when we can have Hector bare his testimony, when he isnt a member... yet.

Investigators that are progressing...
Hector
Kid is great. He comes to church the whole time, he helps us with other investigators, and he knows it is all true. We are hoping to have his baptism on the 21st. The change he has had between the first lesson and now is night and day. Testimony building for sure.
Familia Leon
They are good too. Not perfect, but they are progressing. They all want to be baptized I think, the problem is getting them to church. People are lazy here and church starts at 8, so it is tough. We expect the family of 5 to get baptized eventually, just need to get them to come to church

Ultimately, the problem is always coming to church. People work all week, and sunday is for sleeping, washing clothes, and playing soccer. People pray and read, they just dont come to church. Anyways, this week was solid. Glad to be finishing my first cambio. Spanish is still coming along, not perfect or anywhere near it, but I try. I find myself thinking in spanish sometimes, and that is weird. Hope all is going well back home. Church is still true and it always will be. Bolivia is great, but different and I have grown a lot. I guess this is it until Monday. Hasta Lunes

Elder Bagley

Here are some fun Bolivian differences
1. They put corn on pizza, and it is actually good
2. When you finish a meal, you says gracias, and everyone says provecho, which means something like enjoy it... even though you are done eating. Dont understand
3. 80% of the cars have names. David, Juan, Reybuchan, weird stuff. No one really knows why, but the cars always have name stickers
4. The houses are always brick, and for some reason they usually only paint the front. Lazy
5. Cars are impatient, and honk always. Every corner they honk so no one stops them.
6. For spanish, if something starts with R or has a RR, it is a Z sound
7. They dont have time really. Church starts 10 minutes late even
8. It really is rice and potatoes and potatoes and rice
9. Cholitas. It is a cultural thing for some women to be cholitas. They usually just speak Quechua, they always always have braided hair, they poop on the ground at times, and they can carry like 100 pounds easy on their packs. Youll see 60 year old women carry 100lb bags of rice up flights of stairs like it is nothing. The music of the cholita is haunting too. They are like the witches of Bolivia
