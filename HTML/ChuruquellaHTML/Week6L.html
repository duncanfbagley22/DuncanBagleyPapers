Week 6 Mission President Letter

Weekly Letter - 09 Sep 2013

Hola Presidente,
    This week was pretty good. It is weird to think that I have almost finished my first cambio, it is like yesterday that I was arriving in Cochabamba. This week we had some ups and downs. One day we walked around for hours, and had appointment after appointment fall through. Another was teaching pretty much the whole day. We have had some decent success with knocking doors, cause references have been few. Yesterday we had a reference of a family though. We took a few members with us to teach, and also a investigator named Hector. Hector even challenged the family to pray, and he isnt even a member yet. It was great to see how he knows it is true. We are pretty confident that he will be baptized on the 21st, which will be my first baptism. We have some other investigators that are progressing, but we have problems getting them to church. They have come before, but it isnt constant. Our church starts at 8, so it is tough to get people there. That is why references are the best, cause the people have friends to go to church with. Overall, pretty solid week. I am finally accustomed to the life here, and I understand mostly everything in the lessons, I just need to get the confidence to speak the spanish that I know. I am starting to love the scriptures more and more too. Every time I read them and study there is something applicable for me in the moment. I am grateful for this first cambio experience that I have had, and I am ready to become a better missionary in my second one. Church is definitely true, and I can see the blessings of the Lord everyday. Thanks for the assignment and everything. Hasta Lunes!
    Elder Bagley

President Dyer Response
