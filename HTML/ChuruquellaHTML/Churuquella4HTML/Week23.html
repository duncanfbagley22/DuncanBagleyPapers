Week 23 (January 6, 2014)

Dos Mil Catorce

Happy New Year!
It is crazy that it is already 2014. I am in my only full year in the mission. New years was uneventful for us. We got home, and ended up falling asleep at like 10. We did make scones the next morning though, so everyone was happy. This week brought something good... Like Dennys´s baptism! It was great. We had to show up to church at 6 to fill and heat the font in time. However, everything worked out and we even had all the youth of the ward there, so that helped. We also had breakfast at his house after. We werent sure that the baptism was going to go through, as his mom is just a little weird, and his real dad was here for the first time in 3 years from Brazil, and we werent sure about him. When I was praying that morning though, a voice told me everything would be ok, so that calmed me down a bit. Everything worked, I baptized him and Pinta confirmed him. It was awesome. Today we went to the 7 cascadas (waterfalls) again and this time we climbed more. Some people even fell all the way in the water, but not me and Pinta, cause we are awesome. There´ll be some good pictures of that eventually, when the other elders pass them to me. As for the other investigators, only Karla is progressing. She´ll be baptized the 25th, and I am pretty confident itll happen. She has a testimony and support of the ward and everything. Dina for some reason didnt come to church yesterday. She is still House Hunting Bolivia, and sometimes that takes times away. We will figure it out though. We met some other cool people this week. Hno Fredy (Oliver´s dad) gave us a reference of someone that used to come to church, but stopped cause they moved. Her name is Cristina and her daughter Estrellita Divina (Divine little star? Bolivians are weird). Anyways, the only hard part is getting her to understand everything. We had to invite her to pray like 4 times cause she didnt understand the question. We also met Yascara (why cant they have normal names). She is really curious. She also has a nice house and makes good food. The BoM sparked her interest. Other than that, we have a few good people, but everyone is traveling, which makes it hard. It is weird that this could be my last week. This is the only area I have ever known. We will find out Sunday whats up. I am excited to see what happens. Love you guys, and hope everything keeps going well. Church is really really really true.
Love,
Elder Bagley
