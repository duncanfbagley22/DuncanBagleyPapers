Week 10 Mission President Letter

Weekly Letter - 31 Mar 2014

Presidente Dyer
    This week was full of ups and downs. I enjoyed having the interview and the conference. We are really trying hard to keep the schedule in the morning, and we have done a pretty good job so far. We are just trying to work hard overall. We are having good success in one side of our area, as we have almost reactivated 3 people and 1 person has a baptismal date. It is a little interesting living with Elder Pinta, as he was my companion before, and now he is in my ward. I did have a difficult moment this week. We studied in the morning on Saturday and everything was normal. In between our language and companion study, I was just thinking too much about not having many baptisms. I was being hard on myself, and I just started to cry. I dont think Elder Muñoz knew what to do, but I just needed to let some things out. At times it is really tough to watch people get all the baptisms, and then they just do whatever they want. At times I lose my patience and wonder when will the success come. Luckily, that day we did have a little success later in the day, meeting with a less active family that wants to come back to church, and it went really well. Our numbers werent as good as we wanted this week, but it has made us more anxious to have good numbers this week. Thank you for your example and the lessons in the conference and interview.
    
    Elder Bagley

President Dyer Response

Gracias Elder Bagley por disfrutarse en la mision mediate un trabajo duro y eficaz. Thanks for being obedient. Gracias por amar a Él Senor. Él le bendecirá segun su obediencia. Él nos ha dicho, "Si me amáis, guardad mis mandamientos."

Presidente Dyer
