Week 1 Mission President Letter

Weekly Letter - 27 Ene 2014

Dear President Dyer
    Well, this was quite a surprising week. As you read in my last email, I was so ready to stay after God answered my prayers. It looked like we were going to have a good week, but then on Wednesday everything changed. When I found out I was getting an emergency cambio it shocked me. I wondered for a while what was the reason for leaving, but I am sure that this is part of God´s plan. Thursday was a hectic day, running around getting ready to leave and making sure Elder Pinta would have stuff to do for a while. I really enjoyed my time with him, he taught me so much and will be a friend for life. Well, I have been here in Jardin 2 for about 3 days now. It is really different from Sucre. From everything I hear and see, it is a great ward for missionary work. The ward mission leader is amazing, along with the bishop. I am really excited to get to work here in El Paso. My comp is Elder Hernandez, and he is a really good missionary. His heart is in the right place and came out for the right reasons. I am sure you know about the problems he had the last 2 cambios with the missionaries here, but we are working with that. I have no problem getting along with him, just that he has problems with other missionaries here. I am sure we will fix things up and he will get along with everyone in no time. I cant really comment on the area, as we havent had much time to work, but I am sure that I will soon see why I need to be here. Thanks for everything that you do. Oh ya, one question. I cant seem to find a definite answer when it comes to how to count lessons and new investigators. The ZLs in Sucre told me that lessons require: a principle, a prayer, and some reading or homework we leave behind. Also, that a new investigator needs to have a lesson to be counted as new. Apparently the other missionaries or the ZLs here have told my companion something different. I just want your definite answer, until then we´ll just use what PMG says. Anyways, I am excited to work this week, I am sure it will be a good week.
    
    Elder Bagley

President Dyer Response

It is described in PME but this is it: A nuevo is one who has been taught a lesson, has received a compromiso from the missionaries, has had a least one prayer with the missionaries and has invited the missionaries back (return visit). PME says that the nuevo must have had a lesson that was openned and closed with a prayer (two prayers). That is the perfect definition but....when we are street contacting and you find someone golden, you will not know it until after it is too late to have a prayer in the street to begin your discussion so.....Sometimes it is OK to have only one prayer. Yes you must have taught a principle to the nuevo inorder to count them as nuevos. That is the easiest part.

Gracias por efectuar la obra de salvacion. Favor de trabajar con los miembros. Referencias a los menos activos es equal como referencia a los que no son miembros. Gracias por lo que hace para Él Señor. Adlente con fe. Le queremos mucho. Que Él Señor le bendiga.

Presidente Dyer
