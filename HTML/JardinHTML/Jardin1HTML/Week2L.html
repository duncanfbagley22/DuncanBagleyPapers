Week 2 Mission President Letter

Weekly Letter - 03 Feb 2014

Dear Presidente Dyer
    This week was pretty good. I think I have finally gotten used to being in El Paso. Jardin is an incredible ward. It is really opened my eyes on how to really have success. The ward mission leader and the bishop are 100% on our side. They call spur of the moment meetings just to find out how they can help, they even offer to come to our district meetings to find out if there is anything they can help in. I heard that a few months ago the assistance in this ward was about 100 more or less, and yesterday we reached 230. The excitement in the ward is contagious. We have contracted a trufi that brings people from El Paso all the way down to the church, and yesterday it was so full that Elder Hernandez and I had to wait to take a different trufi. I have been told by Elder Hernandez that there has been talk of closing the area of Jardin 2, and I cant imagine why. There is so much this area has to offer, we just need a little bit of time to get things going as Elder Hernandez wasnt able to work much when Elder Gutierrez broke his foot. We are starting to find some more success, and I am excited about this area. As far as my companion goes, we have our ups and our downs. We have never really fought or anything, but there are still things we need to iron out. I came into the area thinking that he needed to change this or that, but I realized that I have a lot to improve too, so I should work of that. We are really different, but we have the same goals. If you have any advice for how to improve a relationship, I would gladly take it. I dont mean to say that we dont get along, just that we need to improve the relationship a little. Thanks for all that you do and thanks for putting in this area.
    
    Elder Bagley

President Dyer Response

Always take a positive attitude with him. Expect that he wants to work but at times does not feel well enough. He will slowly change. He has very few way to control the situation and being sick is one of them. Give a little more credit and look for the good that he is doing.

Favor de cumplir el cuestionario hoy. Le doy una media hora más para cumplirlo. Favor de escribirme una breve historia espiritual esta semana, nada mas. Le queremos mucho. Que Él Señor le bendiga.

Presidente Dyer
