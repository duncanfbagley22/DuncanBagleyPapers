Week 3 Mission President Letter

Weekly Letter - 10 Feb 2014

Dear President Dyer
    This week was a long one, but I learned a lot. This week we had a lot of things happen where it made it hard to go out and work, things we didnt have control over. Whether it was going with a recent convert to the hospital because no one else would help him, Elder Hernandez being sick, or other things, the work was impeded. However, we are starting to find some more success here. Obviously, with what has happened in Elder Hernandez´s family this week, he has been distracted, and with good reason. He wants to work, but his mind has been focused on how his mom and sister are doing in the hospital. I have been trying this week to distract him so he doesnt worry about it, but it is still on his mind. We have been finding a little bit of success here, but I think this week we can really make some results. Elder Hernandez and I have definitely had some rough parts, it isnt that he and I are against each other or that we dont want to work, but I have never had a companion that is so polar opposite from me in personality. At first I was focusing on what he needed to change and what he can do better, but after lots of pondering I realized that I have a lot to fix as well. I realized that I was losing my pacience for trivial things, and that I just need to love him. We are still trying to work out some things, but the relationship is a whole lot better than last week. We are different, but our goals are the same. We are receiving a ridiculous amount of help from our ward. It is what a ward focused on missionary work should be like. The bishop went as far as sending us a text message yesterday thanking us and just being a great bishop. The ward mission leader helps us out as well. They are examples of how members should help. I think I am accustomed to Jardin 2 now and I know what we have going on, so I feel like this should be a great week. Thanks for all that you do.
    
    Elder Bagley

President Dyer Response

Gracias Elder Bagely, le queremos mucho. Que Él Señor le bendiga.

Presidente Dyer
