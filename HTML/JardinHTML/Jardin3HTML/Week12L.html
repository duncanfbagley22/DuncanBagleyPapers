Week 12 Mission President Letter

Weekly Letter - 14 Abr 2014

Dear President Dyer
    This week was up and down. We had a ward activity that kind of ruined the whole week. Up until Friday, the week was going pretty well, but Friday we had the activity. We were asked to go to the chapel to set up a stage for the activity, and we ended up doing it just as the missionaries. Our ward mission leader left us waiting there for 2 hours for him to come back so we could finish. We spent the entire day either doing service or waiting, and then at night we had the activity, which started late, so a lot of people just left anyways. We were pretty frustrated as we lost the entire day and spent saturday doing the things we needed to be doing on Friday. For some reason, our ward´s support for us has gone downhill. It seems that as we have become better, more obedient missionaries in the ward, the support has gone down. We arent having ward counsel, and when we try to talk to the WML about it, he basically just says everything is our fault, from not having ward counsel to the home teaching not being done. It has been a bit difficult for these reasons, but the success is still gradually coming. We found out yesterday that one of our investigators that was praying but not receiving her answer, received an answer and now wants to be baptized. It made me and Elder Muñoz so happy we ran away from the lesson jumping for joy. The week was up and down, but we are still making progress. Thanks for everything
    
    Elder Bagley

President Dyer Response

Sorry about the ward activity. Things will get better. Your Bishop is overwhelmed and he is looking for others to delegate to. He does not have them. He is much more frustrated than you are.

Favor de estudiar El Libro de Mormon. Gracias por ser discipulo de Cristo. El Señor esta complacido con su servicio, sino Él Espera más de nosotros. Favor de fijar metas más enfocadas en como ayudar Él Salvador. Que tenga el Espiritu Santo siempre.

Presidente Dyer
