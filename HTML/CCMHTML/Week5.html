Week 5 (July 18, 2013)

Wilford Bagley

Hola erbody, I dont really know where to start. This last week has been pretty busy. We have gone through a new group of missionaries and now we are the oldest. Pretty crazy stuff. It is crazy around here now. The CCM is usually supposed to hold no more than 120, with 164 absolute maximum. There are 171 this week. It is crowded. Our room has 8 beds now instead of 6. 8 people in one room that is smaller than my college dorm. The mission age change really did a lot. I will miss the old roomies, but not as much as the first ones. They were just too quiet. I got along really well with some of the latinos from across the hall. I kept telling them that I was gonna get 2000 baptisms on saturday, so they either called me big bagley, or Wilford Bagley like Wilford Woodruff. Obviously, the biggest part of the week was that we got to go tracting. They put us with latino companions, so I went with Elder Ramos my roomie from Columbia. We went to a place called Santa Anita, a little suburb of Lima. It was definitely a different experience. Just getting out there and only speaking spanish for 4 hours was something else. We mostly hung around a park. It was crazy how welcoming people were. We would just start talking to someone and 90% of the time theyd be ok with us teaching them for 10-20 minutes. In 4 hours we got 8 contacts, taught about 4 lessons, handed out a bunch of pamphlets, and placed one BoM. I have a bunch of experiences to share, but I will just stick with the 3 best/worst ones. First cool thing was with a guy named Pedro. We had been teaching another old guy, 66 he was turning 67 a few days later and he kept reminding us. He hated the USA, so that was fun. He was devout Catholic, but he listened to us. We gave him a Plan of Salvation and Restoration pamphlet and figured we were done with him. We went over and started teaching Pedro the same things. Out of no where the other guy came back and started asking me about communicating with God and kept telling me that we cant directly communicate through. So Im telling him that we can and stuff, and then Pedro jumps in and tells him that through prayer and prophets we communicate with God. It was cool that a guy we just barely taught was teaching someone else. As we left they kept talking for 15-20 minutes. Second was this crazy guy. No more than 2 teeth, like 70 years old. I thought everything was good, but then he just kept talking and talking. It went on for like 30 minutes. After we left I asked Ramos what he said and apparently he was just calling us liars the whole time. Definitely didnt understand that part. Oh well. Lastly was our last lesson. I told Ramos that I wasnt gonna let anything stop me from placing a BoM. We saw this family sitting on the curb. We talked to them for a while and found out missionaries had given them a BoM before, but they lost it. We talked about the Restoration and finally I got to place a BoM. I told the dad that a good Chapter was 3 Ne 11, so he ripped a piece of newspaper off and put it in the BoM. It felt so good knowing that I couldve been changing someones life right then. It was a really tiring day, but it is more satisfying than staying in a room. Other than tracting, important things are that I am not DL anymore, sad day. Our new roomies are pretty cool. 3.5 latinos and 2.5 americans. There is a latino from Vegas that speaks both. One of the 2 white guys went to bingham, and he is gonna play Linebacker for Boise State after the mission. He is huge. All are nice though. Our class has been trying to speak spanish most the time, but that has only kinda worked. Spanish is definitely improving, like always. We got our missionary coaching from our teacher this week, but just like last time, they told us that me and christensen are doing great and they cant see any apparent flaws, even though there are definitely some in everyone. It makes me feel good that I am doing well. We learned this week that emails can help out those that read them at home so pay attention. The things I have really been hit with this week are that the gospel is simple, and that I have the power to change these peoples lives forever. The gospel isnt really as hard as people make it out to be. We just need faith in Christ, belief in the scriptures, and belief in prophets, past and present. Everything else is just based off those things. When it comes to the people, I have got to realize that I am meeting them for a reason. Even if they dont imediately accept, I am planting a seed. I am trying to see people as Christ would, just pure love. Everyone here is so nice, so I can just be open about the gospel with them. Spanish is tough at times, but as long as I try, my mouth will be filled. So ya... church is true. More true than anything. It is weird that I have 12 days left. Tuesday morning I fly out. There is a chance I can call home tuesday or wednesday, but I am not sure. We have heard we might just fly to La Paz and then drive to Cochabamba. Not sure how I feel about that. This is all for this week. Love you all whoever you are and I know this church is true. Love, Elder Bagley P.S my district is the best. Me Christensen and 8 Hermanas going to chiclayo. They are: Hermanas Ashley Larsen, Sunny Grant, Ashley Hanson, Michele Reid, Kimber Hess, Maria Fuller, Kaylee Carver and Rachel Heinrich. Heinrich says she recognizes you(mom and dad) not sure why. Anyways, they are all great. P.S.S me and elder price are holding a districts christmas tree for ransom, but they dont know it is us. We have been leaving notes and stuff. Pretty sly. Also, I am best friends with like 8 teachers now. Latinos are the best
