Week 4 Mission President Letter

Weekly Letter - 17 Jun 2014

Dear President Dyer
    This week just kind of flew by. It is crazy how fast these four weeks have gone, and as of tomorrow, I will be working as a secretary all on my own. I feel ready, but at the same time a little nervous because I wont have Elder Gallegos to back me up. I have got the hang of all the basics, but the fine details are what I am missing. I think a lot of it will just come with time and experience. This week we were able to go teach a family that is a recently activated father and his nonmember wife. She always attends church and likes it, but she hasnt been baptized yet. I believe that she can be a baptism very soon as she has felt the spirit and knows what she needs to do. I think a lack of follow up is to blame for why she hasnt progressed more. Even though I havent had the opportunity to teach investigators a lot here in my time in the office, there are possibilities here in Cobija. Yesterday we had a great lesson in Elders Quorum about magnifying our priesthood. That is something I have come to understand just a little bit better during my time here in the mission. I am going to have 1 year in the mission this week, and I am still amazed at how fast the time has gone by. I look forward to take over 100% of my job here in this coming week. Thank you for everything.
    
    Elder Bagley

President Dyer Response

Gracias por sus muchas cartas. Esta es la ultima carta de mi. No habrá más opportunidad de escribir. Leeré la carta del lunes siguiente pero no le escribiré. Le quiero mucho. Nos comunicaremos por facebook en el futuro. Gracias por ser siervo fiel del Señor.

Presidente Dyer
