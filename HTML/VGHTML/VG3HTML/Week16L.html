Week 16 Mission President Letter

Weekly Letter - 06 Abr 2014

Dear President Hansen,
    I don’t know what happened, but I guess my letter from last week never sent. Sorry about that. Anyways, this week was difficult. We spent a lot of time doing administrative stuff. We looked for houses in Amancayas, gave baptismal interviews, and had stuff like that. While we might not have had a ton of success as far as the area, but I learned a whole lot. I really liked the emphasis on the Book of Mormon. It really does convert people, and those that don’t read aren’t truly converted. We’ve been making sure that we explain it and give the people an opportunity to read it every lesson. I know it’s converted me, so I need to help others be converted by it. I really really enjoyed general conference as well. I did what you asked and wrote down some questions for conference. I definitely got my answer. We were sitting there listening to Pres. Uchtdorf’s talk in priesthood session and I really enjoyed what he was saying. However, about half way through I just got an impression that told me “hey, this is your answer, pay attention”. When he said “many things that can be counted, don’t count and many things that can’t be counted, do count” as well as “many times the service that counts most will only will seen by God” or something like that. It just hit me when he said that. It’s been hard to not find any success here in Villa Graciela, but I realized I have gained many things that can’t be measured, such as a much stronger testimony. I sometimes question what I’m supposed to do here in this area, but I know I will come out more like the person God wants me to be. I found some scriptures this week that helped me feel better. In D and C 111: 1-2 I really felt like God was speaking to me. Thanks for everything, let me know if you need anything from us.
    
    Elder Bagley

President Hansen Response

So glad to hear Gen. Conf. was meaningful to you.
How is your investigator I met after the Noche Fogonera (Jose?).
Thanks for helping your zone place greater emphasis on achieving that investigators read more from the BOM.
