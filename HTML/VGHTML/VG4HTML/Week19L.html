Week 19 Mission President Letter

Weekly Letter - 27 Abr 2014

Dear President Hansen,
    This was a pretty good week. As far as the area goes, it was awesome. There were so many things that happened that were huge blessings. Many people that Elder Hansen and I contacted and looked for forever, decided to receive us and we know have a good group of investigators. It was as if all of our hard work was in vain last transfer, and then this week we saw all of the fruits. I'm really enjoying being with Elder Bradford. He's a hard worker and great missionary. He's already jumped in and doing everything he can as zone leader. I'm sure this will be a great transfer. We're also starting to see some good progress here. We have a family (mother and daughter) that are progressing well and hopefully can get baptized this month. We are having a lot of success in finding families, and the Lord is really blessing us. We met a man a few weeks ago that we contacted in the street at literally 8:55pm. We were able to teach him finally yesterday, and we found out that he's been reading the pamphlet and is really interested. The blessings keep on coming and it's great. As far as the zone, we've got a lot of room to grow. At the beginning of the week we were really discouraged with the zone. We had a lot of problems (people showing up 30min late to conference, people not going to conference, etc). We decided that we're ready for the challenge though. We've started to create an attack plan to hopefully help the zone grow. We're working hard with the DLs. We're a little worried about them, but they all have potential. Hopefully the plans that we have can help the zone to grow. They're great missionaries, they're just not super focused for right now. I'm sure that Elder Bradford and I can get things going here, with our area and the zone. Thanks for everything and let us know if you need anything.
    
    Elder Bagley

President Hansen Response

I love how you are focusing on strengthening your DL's, and on how well you and Elder Bradford are working together. Thanks, Elder Bagley. You're awesome.
