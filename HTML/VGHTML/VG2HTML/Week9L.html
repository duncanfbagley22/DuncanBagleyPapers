Week 9 Mission President Letter

Weekly Letter - 18 Feb 2014

Dear President Hansen,
    The recent weeks have been a little bit frustrating. We've been working really hard, being obedient, but nothing is really progressing. We've tried talking to everyone, going through the references we have, and even going to all the less actives with a member in the ward offering free liahonas and even fruit. The area is in a bit of a drought. However, this last weekend starting to show some signs of progress. This last saturday was really good. We found an investigator the week before through knocking doors, and we had an appointment for saturday. We called and confirmed and we were able to teach him all of the first lesson. As we were ending, he got a call from his mom and she needed help bringing in groceries. We went outside, helped with the groceries, and afterwards we were able to teach the mom and the older brother. They were a little bit familiar with the church and seem great. After studying ch 9 of PMG, I learned (or relearned) that the people we inicially contact might not be the chosen people of the Lord. We saw that to be true 4 or 5 times this week. We just need to try to teach everyone we see and contact their friends and family. I liked how in ch9 it says that we need to find ourselves in the "sendero de aquellos que estan preparadas". The path is just a little different than we think sometimes. As far as the intercambios this week... First I went with Elder Olson. He's obviously a great missionary. I learned a lot from him, especially the way he asks questions in lessons. We also had intercambio with Elder Torres/Gonzales. They're probably our biggest worry in the zone. The intercambio went a lot better than expected. I went with Elder Torres and at least when he was with me he was a great missionary. Elder Hansen said the same thing about Elder Gonzales. We're not sure how they do on their own, but at least with us they did well. Elder Hansen and I are really working to get the zone going, so we're just trying to think of ways to help. I'm excited for the zone conference this week. Thank you, and let me know if there is anything you think I need to improve.
    
    Elder Bagley

President Hansen Response

Thanks for your excellent letter, and for your perseverance in the face of little success despite your diligent efforts. You will see the blessings in time.
