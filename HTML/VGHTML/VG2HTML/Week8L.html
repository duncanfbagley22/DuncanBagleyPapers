Week 8 Mission President Letter

Weekly Letter - 09 Feb 2014

Dear President Hansen,
    I just found out that the letter that I wrote for last week didnt end up getting sent, so I'm sorry for that. This week was good and difficult as well. We've had a bit of a drought recently with new investigators, so we've focused on that. We set contacting goals everyday and worked hard to reach them. We're trying to be creative and use all the possible ways we can think of to contact. I was able to go on an intercambio with Elder Prescott this week. He is a great elder and just always has a great attitude. I learned a lot from him. With elder hansen, yesterday we watched a mormon message with some recent converts. It's called "Good things to come" and it's Jeffrey R Holland. I really like the message as it reminds us to be patient as some blessings come now, some come later, and some not till heaven. I really realized that that is true. On Thursday we worked super hard. I usually feel like if we work hard, something really good will happen that day. Thursday was just rough all around. However, as we continued working, the blessings came. Yesterday we had a cool experience. While walking up the street, we overheard a man telling his daughter how we were called elders. We quickly went over and contacted him. I guess his cousin is a bishop in Quillacollo and he used to be an investigator. He said he really would like to go back to church and learn more. It's always great to basically have someone contact us. However, during that moment, another person we didnt know came up and contacted us. We also talked to him afterwards. Literally in 15 minutes, we met 2 people that were interested in the gospel and afterwards we just stood there and realized how the Lord was blessing us for our work. Elder Hansen is really helping me out a lot and he's a great companion. Thank you for everything, and let me know if we can do anything to help you.
    
    Elder Bagley

President Hansen Response

After the trial of our faith, the miracle comes. You have experienced that once again.
