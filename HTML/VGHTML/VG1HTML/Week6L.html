Week 6 Mission President Letter

Weekly Letter - 26 Ene 2014

Dear President Hansen,
    This week was a little tough. After last week went so well for us as a companionship, this week was a little bit rougher. We had a lot of appointments fall through. It was tough to not get a little discouraged, but I know we learned a lot. We worked hard and finally got someone to church. We're starting to find some better investigators too. The future looks bright here, it's just that we have to push through the rough times we have had recently. I'm going to miss having Elder Laidlaw a lot. We got along great and he helped a lot. Elder Hansen will be a great companion though. He's a great missionary and we already know each other pretty well. The zone got a lot of good misisonaries, so things are looking good. I'm glad that I've been able to learn so much and grow so much in this last cambio. Probably our best moment of the week was Saturday. Earlier in the week, a married couple of recent converts was going through a lot of problems. The dad kept threatening to leave the church and stuff like that. However, Saturday the elders in the other area had a baptism where one of this couple's best friends was baptized. They attended and you could tell that they could feel the spirit. They told us how they remembered their baptism and house it was and the spirit that was there. Since then you can see their improvement and everything is getting better. This ward is really special, and I'm really glad to be here. I've learned and have a testimony of the transfers being inspired, so I know that everything will work out like it should. Thank you for everything, and let me know if there is anything you need from me.
    
    Elder Bagley

President Hansen Response

I'm hoping you can help Elder Hansen become a great zone leader quickly as he doesn't have much time left. I know you can, and that you can have success together and help your zone to push themselves to higher levels.
